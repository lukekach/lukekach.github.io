3 Point Attempts

Perhaps the most major apparent change over the past 20 seasons has been the usage of the three point shot. As analytics have taken over the game, the simple fact that 3 is greater than 2 has lead to teams taking more three pointers. But just how drastic has this change been? To get a better look, I will now graph the NBA's average 3 point attempts per game over the years to see just how much three point usage has increased year over year. To do this, I will use a library called matplotlib. Matplotlib is a very useful library in python that makes graphing and plotting very easy. To learn more about plotting with matplotlib you can follow this link : https://www.w3schools.com/python/matplotlib_plotting.asp.

As you can see from the graph, it is clear that there has been a tremendous shift towards the three point shot league-wide over these years. In 2000-01, teams were shooting an average of below 15 three pointers per game. In 2018-19 that average was all the way up over 32 threes per game. There seems to have been a somewhat steady increase at the start then a huge upward trajectory starting around the 2012-13 season that has continued since then.

Now that it has been established that teams have been shooting an increasing number of threes year over year, I want to see if this increase in attempts has effected efficiency at all. To do this I will make a similar plot to the one above but instead graph the average three point percentages over time. My hypothesis is that there will be a slight increase over time but nothing nearly as drastic as the plot off attempts.

import numpy as np

seasons_n=['2012-13', '2013-14', '2014-15', '2015-16', '2016-17', '2017-18',
       '2018-19']

winp=[]

#Saves win% and 3PA for teams in range of years
for j in seasons_n:
  y= tab.loc[tab['SEASON'] == j]
  for k in y['3PA']:
    threes.append(k)
  for l in y['WIN%']:
    winp.append(l)


threes=np.asarray(threes)
winp=np.asarray(winp)

#plots points
plt.plot(threes, winp, 'o')

#makes and plots regression
m, b = np.polyfit(threes, winp, 1)
plt.plot(threes, m*threes+b)
plt.xlabel('3PA per game', fontsize=14)
plt.ylabel('Win Percentage', fontsize=14)
plt.show()
print("m = ",m)

m =  0.00558080400736805

This graph confirms my hypothesis as it shows that there was a slight positive correlation between 3PA per game and win percentage over the years of 2012-2019 as the leagu-wide shift towards the three point shot really took shape. The regression line had an m of 0.00558080400736805 which means that for every 3PA per game your team has, their winning percentage should go up 0.558080400736805%.

Pace of Play

Another aspect of the NBA that is widely considered to have changed is the overall pace of play. The common belief is that teams in today's NBA play at a much faster pace and get shots up much earlier in the shot clock than they did in the early 2000's. The best way to measure pace of play in by analyzing the Field Goal Attempts per game. This is because if you are playing faster, then you are getting more possessions per game and thus, more shots per game.

The first thing to look at when considering the overall pace of play is look at the overall league wide average FGA per game over time similarly to how we examined the 3 point attempts earlier.

Warriors impact on NBA

The one team that is largely credited with this increase in three point usage and pace is the Golden State Warriors. In the mid to late 2010s, the Warriors were by far the best team in the NBA winning 4 Western Conference Championships, 3 NBA Titles, and setting the single season wins record going 73-9. The NBA is commonly referred to as a "copy-cat" league implying that teams try to replicate the styles and strategies of the most successful teams. I will now test this theory by seeing how the Warriors compared with the rest of the league in pace and three point shot percentage over time. To do this I will use the main DataFrame we have been using to create two new DataFrames: one with only the Warriors teams and one with every team besides the Warriors. I will then plot the three point percent percentages over time for both the warriors and the rest of the league over time.

Which teams committed most to new style of play?

A few obstacles here are that 3 franchises have different names in 2018-19 than they did in 2012-13. The Charlotte Bobcats became the Charlotte Hornets, the New Orleans Hornets became the New Orleans Pelicans, and the Los Angeles Clippers became the LA CLippers (they just abbreviated the city). Since there were only three changes over this time period, I am able to handle them each individually. The other 27 teams are cycled through and the differences are calculated, stored, and plotted.

Conclusions

This deep dive into how the NBA has changed from the 2000-01 season through the 2018-19 season has uncovered a few conclusions we can now make. First off, it is clear that the three point shot has indeed taken over the league. Not only have teams increased the number of threes they shoot per game dramatically, but they have also increased the percentage of shots they take that are threes. Additionally, the pace of play has increased throughout the league as teams are getting more and more possessions and attempting more shots per game.
Moreover there is truth to the belief that the NBA is a "copy-cat" league as we were able to see the Warriors find success in a new style of play and the rest of the league try to replicate that style in response.
Finally, we were able to see that the level of commitment to this new style of a faster pace and more threes has varied between teams but they have all increased their three point usage in some way. The shift towards this style of play has shown no signs of slowing down and will likely continue to be the main style of the game until a team decides to try a new style and finds success in that style.
