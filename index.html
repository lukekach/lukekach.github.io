Introduction

The NBA has and continues to be one of the most popular sports leagues in America, and around the world. With a never ending flow of exciting young talent and some of the most loyal fanbases in all of sports the NBA continues to have success attracting fans growing its popularity. One thing that has always stood out to me as a huge NBA fan, is how different the games look now compared to how they looked in old hihglights I watch on YouTube. There are many aspects of today's game that people love, and some things that people dislike. The purpose of this project is to dive deep into NBA team statistics from 2000 to 2019 and analyze how the game has changed in terms of pace, style of play, and more.

The data set used in this project can be found at https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/mharvnek/nba-team-stats-00-to-18 .

Getting the Data

To get the data in an organized, easy to read table, I make use of the Pyhton library "Pandas" to read in the csv nicely and store it in a DataFrame. Pandas makes organizing and working with data much easier. You can learn more about pandas here. While the data was set up nicely, there was some tidying I needed to do before getting started. The dataset contains data from both the 2019-20 and 2020-21 NBA seasons. As you may remember, both of these seasons were heavily affected by COVID and were shorter than the normal 82 game NBA season. Because of this, I decided to drop those two seasons from the DataFrame and only focus on the seasons 2000-21 through 2018-19. While the last two season could potentially have useful data, it is possible that it could be skewed since not every team played the same amount of games and there were a ton of COVID cases that prevented players from playing. Additionally, these two seasons were played mostly in front of limited capacity crowds or no crowds at all which could make these seasons outliers. Thus, I use pandas to drop those two seasons out of the DataFrame.

3 Point Attempts

Perhaps the most major apparent change over the past 20 seasons has been the usage of the three point shot. As analytics have taken over the game, the simple fact that 3 is greater than 2 has lead to teams taking more three pointers. But just how drastic has this change been? To get a better look, I will now graph the NBA's average 3 point attempts per game over the years to see just how much three point usage has increased year over year. To do this, I will use a library called matplotlib. Matplotlib is a very useful library in python that makes graphing and plotting very easy. To learn more about plotting with matplotlib you can follow this link : https://www.w3schools.com/python/matplotlib_plotting.asp.

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import matplotlib.ticker as mticker
#Get list of seasons
seasons = tab['SEASON'].unique()

#Reverses list to put seasons in chronological order
seasons = np.flip(seasons)

av=[]

#Cycles through seasons and calculates average 3PA per season
for j in seasons:
  y= tab.loc[tab['SEASON'] == j, '3PA']

  tot=0
  for p in y:
    tot=tot+p
  av.append((tot/len(y)))

#plots graph
plt.plot(seasons,av)

plt.xlabel('Season', fontsize=14)
plt.xticks(np.arange(0, len(seasons)+1, 3))
plt.ylabel('Average 3PA', fontsize=14)
plt.show

As you can see from the graph, it is clear that there has been a tremendous shift towards the three point shot league-wide over these years. In 2000-01, teams were shooting an average of below 15 three pointers per game. In 2018-19 that average was all the way up over 32 threes per game. There seems to have been a somewhat steady increase at the start then a huge upward trajectory starting around the 2012-13 season that has continued since then.

Now that it has been established that teams have been shooting an increasing number of threes year over year, I want to see if this increase in attempts has effected efficiency at all. To do this I will make a similar plot to the one above but instead graph the average three point percentages over time. My hypothesis is that there will be a slight increase over time but nothing nearly as drastic as the plot off attempts.

The next thing I want to explore is the success of this new strategy of shooting more threes. To get a broad look at this, I will use matplotlib to make a scatter plot of teams win percentage and three point attempts per game. I will use another library called numpy to calculate a line of regression to get a better look at the correlation of 3PA to win percentage. For this I will only look at the seasons of 2012-13 to 2018-19 as these were the seasons that this shift toward the three point shot really started to take shape.

import numpy as np

seasons_n=['2012-13', '2013-14', '2014-15', '2015-16', '2016-17', '2017-18',
       '2018-19']

threes=[]
winp=[]

#Saves win% and 3PA for teams in range of years
for j in seasons_n:
  y= tab.loc[tab['SEASON'] == j]
  for k in y['3PA']:
    threes.append(k)
  for l in y['WIN%']:
    winp.append(l)


threes=np.asarray(threes)
winp=np.asarray(winp)

#plots points
plt.plot(threes, winp, 'o')

#makes and plots regression
m, b = np.polyfit(threes, winp, 1)
plt.plot(threes, m*threes+b)
plt.xlabel('3PA per game', fontsize=14)
plt.ylabel('Win Percentage', fontsize=14)
plt.show()
